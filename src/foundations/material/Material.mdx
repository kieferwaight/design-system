# Material Foundation

Apple's translucent system surfaces. A **material** is a blurred, adaptive fill that samples the content behind it — the reason toolbars, sheets, sidebars, and notification banners feel like they float *above* the page rather than sitting on a flat color.

The design system defines materials by **thickness**, from most see-through to most opaque:

Each `.material-*` class applies three coordinated rules:
1. An adaptive **translucent fill** (`--material-*` using `light-dark()` syntax).
2. A matched **backdrop blur** (tied to the `--blur-*` elevation tiers).
3. A hairline **top highlight** (`--material-border`) to catch light on the container's glass rim.

```html
<div class="material-thick">...toolbar content...</div>
```

---

## Vibrancy & Foreground Contrast

Content placed *on* a material should leverage the standard semantic foreground labels defined by the color foundation.

These are pre-tuned to achieve AA/AAA contrast ratios when layered over the adaptive blurred backgrounds in both light and dark appearances.

---

## Performance Considerations

> [!WARNING]
> Because backdrop-filter blurring is GPU-heavy during transitions and scroll animations, use materials deliberately on macro floating containers rather than nesting them repeatedly.
